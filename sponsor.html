Gold Rush Robotics is a student-led club at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte. Our mission is to bring together undergraduate students of all skill levels who share a passion for robotics. We provide a collaborative space for students to learn, grow, and network all while gaining valuable hands-on experience that will lead to future success in the industry.

What we do

To accommodate both experienced and inexperienced students, Gold Rush Robotics is organized into two sections: skill building and competition. The skill building programs offers activities to introduce club members to the basics of robotics. Such as: workshops led by experienced club members, Micromouse maze and line follower challenges, Arduino kits, and mini Battle Bots.

Our competition program gives club members the opportunity to compete against other universities from across the country. We have teams participating in both IEEE Southeast Con Main Hardware Competition and IEEE Southeast Con Open Hardware Competition. These teams design and build a new robot every year to solve a set of challenges and then take their robot to competitions.
